Dr. Seuss

Fun Fact:
Although an author of children's books,
Dr. Seuss ironically had no kids.
He used to say:
"You have ‘em; I’ll entertain ‘em."

Life

  • Born in Springfield, Massachusetts on March 2, 1904 as Theodor Seuss Giesel
  • Adpoted the name "Dr. Seuss" during his universty studies in Dartmouth College, where he graduated in 1925
  • After graduating in Dartmouth, he entered Lincoln College, Oxford intending to earn a PhD in English literature
  • There, he met Helen Palmer, who encouraged him to give up becoming an English teacher
  • Left Oxford in 1927 to focus on his career as cartoonist and illustrator
  • Worked for publications including Vanity Fair, Life, and advertising campaigns like Flit and Standard Oil
  • Published his first children's book And to Think That I Saw It on Mulberry Street in 1937
  • During World War II, worked in an animation department of the US Army producing short films, including Design for Death, which later won the 1947 Academy Award for Documentary Feature
  • After the war, focused on children's books, writing classics such as If I Ran the Zoo (1950), Horton Hears a Who! (1955)
  • He published over 60 books during his career
  • Died September 24, 1991
